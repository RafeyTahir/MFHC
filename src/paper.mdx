## Abstract
<div style="text-align: justify;">
We propose a hierarchical model-free control framework for generating stable limit cycles in underactuated nonlinear systems. The approach combines a high-level reference generator with a robust adaptive low-level controller that requires only joint measurements, eliminating the need for explicit system models. The method ensures stability while producing periodic motion in both actuated and unactuated coordinates. The framework is validated through hardware experiments on a rotary inverted pendulum and a VTOL aircraft model, and simulations on additional benchmarks including the cart-pole and leg–foot systems, demonstrating strong robustness and performance compared to existing model-free approaches.
</div>
</HighlightedSection>

## Problem Statement and Proposed Approach

Underactuated mechanical systems are challenging to control because they possess fewer actuators than degrees of freedom. This work proposes a dual model-free control framework with a robust adaptive controller that extends existing model-free approaches to underactuated systems while preserving robustness and optimality properties. The effectiveness of the proposed strategy is demonstrated on several benchmark systems, including the rotary inverted pendulum, VTOL aircraft model, cart–pole, and leg–foot model, showing improved robustness, simplicity, and performance compared to other model-free control techniques.

### Limit-Cycle Generation

A limit cycle is a closed trajectory in the phase plane of a nonlinear dynamical system that attracts nearby trajectories, producing stable periodic motion. Designing limit cycles enables systems to generate desired repetitive behaviors, such as stable locomotion in walking robots. Due to these properties, limit cycle dynamics are widely applied in robotics, mechanical systems, electrical engineering, power electronics, and chemical processes.
## Framework Overview

The control architecture is organized hierarchically. The **high-level controller** generates reference trajectories that induce a desired periodic motion, while the **low-level controller** ensures robust tracking of these trajectories despite uncertainty and underactuation.

## Results Implementation Examples

To demonstrate the effectiveness and generality of the proposed hierarchical model-free control framework, the controller is implemented on four benchmark **underactuated mechanical systems**:

- Rotary Inverted Pendulum (RIP)
- Acrobot
- Segway
- Leg–Foot model on deformable ground

These systems represent a wide class of nonlinear underactuated dynamics and provide a comprehensive validation of the proposed control framework.

# 1. Rotary Inverted Pendulum (RIP)

<img
  src="/Setup_RIP.png"
  alt="Framework overview"
  style="display:block; max-width:900px; width:100%; margin: 0 auto;"
/>


The **Rotary Inverted Pendulum (RIP)** consists of a horizontally rotating arm driven by a motor and a pendulum attached at the end of the arm. The rotary arm is actuated while the pendulum motion remains unactuated, making the system underactuated and strongly nonlinear.

The dynamics of the system can be written as

$$
\begin{aligned}
(m_{p}L^{2}_{r} + J_{r})\ddot{\theta}_{a} - \frac{1}{2}m_{p}L_{p}\ddot{\theta}_{u} &= \tau, \\
\frac{1}{2}m_{p}L_{p}\ddot{\theta}_{a} + \left(J_{p} + \frac{1}{4}m_{p}L^{2}_{p}\right)\ddot{\theta}_{u}
+ \frac{m_{p}L_{p}g\theta_{u}}{2} &= 0 .
\end{aligned}
$$

### Parameter Specification of RIP

| Parameter | Description | Value | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|
| $L_{p}$ | Length of the Pendulum | 0.129 | m |
| $L_{r}$ | Length of the Rotary Arm | 0.085 | m |
| $m_{p}$ | Mass of the Pendulum | 0.024 | kg |
| $J_{p}$ | Pendulum Inertia | $3.3\times10^{-5}$ | kg·m² |
| $J_{r}$ | Rotary Arm Inertia | $5.7\times10^{-5}$ | kg·m² |
| $A$ | Oscillation amplitude | 5 | deg |
| $T$ | Oscillation period | 2 | s |
| $g$ | Gravitational acceleration | 9.8 | m/s² |

# 2. Acrobot

The **Acrobot** is a two-link planar robot where only the second joint is actuated. The first joint remains unactuated, making the system a classical benchmark for nonlinear underactuated control.

The dynamics can be expressed as

$$
\begin{aligned}
d_{11}\ddot{q}_{u}+d_{12}\ddot{q}_{a} + h_{u} + \phi_{u} &=0, \\
d_{21}\ddot{q}_{u}+d_{22}\ddot{q}_{a} + h_{a} + \phi_{a} &= \tau .
\end{aligned}
$$

where

$$
\begin{aligned}
d_{11}&= m_{1}l_{c1}^{2} + m_{2}(l^{2}_{1} + l^{2}_{c2} +2l_{1}l_{c2}\cos(q_{a})) + I_{1} + I_{2},\\
d_{22}&= m_{2}l^{2}_{c2}+I_{2},\\
d_{12}&= m_{2}(l_{c2}^{2} + l_{1}l_{c2}\cos(q_{a})) + I_{2},\\
d_{21}&= m_{2}(l^{2}_{c2} + l_{1}l_{c2}\cos(q_{a})) + I_{2},\\
h_{1} &= -m_{2}l_{1}l_{c2}\sin(q_{a})\dot{q}_{a}^{2} -2m_{2}l_{1}l_{c2}\sin(q_{a})\dot{q}_{a}\dot{q}_{u},\\
h_{2} &= m_{2}l_{1}l_{c2}\sin(q_{a})\dot{q}_{u}^{2},\\
\phi_{1} &= (m_{1}l_{c1}+m_{2}l_{1})g\cos(q_{u}) + m_{2}l_{c2}g\cos(q_{u}+q_{a}),\\
\phi_{2} &= m_{2}l_{c2}g\cos(q_{u}+q_{a}).
\end{aligned}
$$

### Parameter Specification of Acrobot

| Parameter | Description | Value | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|
| $m_{1}$ | Mass of the first link | 1 | kg |
| $m_{2}$ | Mass of the second link | 1 | kg |
| $l_{1}$ | Length of link 1 | 1 | m |
| $l_{2}$ | Length of link 2 | 1 | m |
| $l_{c1}$ | COM of link 1 | 0.5 | m |
| $l_{c2}$ | COM of link 2 | 0.5 | m |
| $I_{1}$ | Inertia of link 1 | 0.083 | kg·m² |
| $I_{2}$ | Inertia of link 2 | 0.33 | kg·m² |
| $g$ | Gravitational acceleration | 9.8 | m/s² |

# 3. Segway

The **Segway system** represents a wheeled inverted pendulum where the wheel motion is actuated while the body pitch evolves as an unactuated coordinate. This system is widely used to study balancing and locomotion control.

The equations of motion are

$$
\begin{aligned}
H_{1}\ddot{x} + (H_{2}\cos\theta)\ddot{\theta} - (H_{6}\sin\theta)\dot{\theta}^{2} &= \tau, \\
(H_{3}\cos\theta)\ddot{x} + H_{4}\ddot{\theta} - H_{5}\sin\theta &= -K\tau .
\end{aligned}
$$

### Parameter Specification of Segway

| Parameter | Description | Value | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|
| $M$ | Mass of robot body | 3.5 | kg |
| $m$ | Mass of wheel | 2 | kg |
| $l$ | Length of leg | 0.2 | m |
| $I_{w}$ | Wheel inertia | 0.0266 | kg·m² |
| $I_{r}$ | Robot body inertia | 0.0043 | kg·m² |
| $r$ | Wheel radius | 0.05 | m |

# 4. Leg–Foot Model on Deformable Ground

The **Leg–Foot model** represents a simplified locomotion system interacting with deformable terrain. The model consists of three coordinates and exhibits complex nonlinear coupling due to ground compliance.

The dynamics are given by

$$
M(q)\ddot{q} + C(q,\dot{q})\dot{q} + H(q) = \tau .
$$

where

$$
q =
\begin{bmatrix}
y & \theta & \alpha
\end{bmatrix}^T
$$

and

$$
M =
\begin{bmatrix}
m_{f}+m_{l}& -l_{cm}m_{l}\sin(\theta+\alpha) & -l_{cm}m_{l}\sin(\theta+\alpha)\\
-l_{cm}m_{l}\sin(\theta+\alpha)&m_{l}l_{cm}^{2}+I_{f}+I_{l}&m_{l}l_{cm}^{2}+I_{l}\\
-l_{cm}m_{l}\sin(\theta+\alpha)&m_{l}l^{2}+I_{l}&m_{l}l^{2}+I_{l}
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
C =
\begin{bmatrix}
0&-l_{cm}m_{l}\cos(\theta+\alpha)(\dot{\theta}+2\dot{\alpha})&-l_{cm}m_{l}\cos(\theta+\alpha)\dot{\alpha}\\
0&0&0\\
0&0&0
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
H =
\begin{bmatrix}
g(m_{f}+m_{l}) + 2ky\\
k\delta^{2}\sin(2\theta)-gl_{cm}m_{l}\sin(\theta+\alpha)\\
-gl_{cm}m_{l}\sin(\theta+\alpha)
\end{bmatrix},
\quad
\tau =
\begin{bmatrix}
0\\
0\\
u
\end{bmatrix}
$$

### Parameter Specification of Leg-Foot Model

| Parameter | Description | Value | Unit |
|---|---|---|---|
| $m_{l}$ | Mass of the leg | 1 | kg |
| $m_{f}$ | Mass of the foot | 0.2 | kg |
| $l$ | Length of the leg | 40 | cm |
| $I_{f}$ | Inertia of foot | 0.0166 | kg·m² |
| $I_{l}$ | Inertia of leg | 0.083 | kg·m² |
| $\delta$ | Distance from ankle to tip | 15 | cm |
| $k$ | Ground stiffness constant | $5\times10^{3}$ | N/m |
